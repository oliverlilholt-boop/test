01 — Risk Framework

Aligned with MITRE ATT&CK and NIST CSF

A structured model for human risk.

Moxso organises human risk across three core dimensions — Behavioral Risk (what people do), Threat Exposure (what targets them), and Inherent Risk (who they are structurally). These map to 16 defined risk categories, aligned with MITRE ATT&CK and NIST CSF. Every score is quantifiable and auditable. This is what makes risk comparable over time and defensible at board level.

R.01
Communication Security
How people use email, chat, and collaboration tools — patterns, exposures, risky behaviors.
R.02
Engagement & Awareness
Participation and measurable retention from security education over time.
R.03
Data Protection
How people handle, share, store, and transfer sensitive information.
R.04
Identity & Access Risk
Authentication methods, permission creep, and access behavior patterns.
R.05
Web & Cloud Usage
Internet and cloud service access patterns, shadow IT, risky service usage.
R.06
Endpoint & Device Security
Device posture, configuration compliance, and physical condition indicators.
R.07
Physical Security
Human interaction with physical spaces, access points, and physical assets.
R.08
Social Engineering Risk
Susceptibility to fear, trust, urgency, and overconfidence manipulation tactics.
R.09
Incident Response Readiness
Reporting behavior, escalation patterns, and participation in IR processes.
R.10
Remote Work Risk
Security behavior outside traditional office environments and network controls.
R.11
Digital Exposure Risk
Publicly available sensitive personal or professional information online.
R.12
Mobile Security
Use and security posture of smartphones and tablets used for work.
R.13
Third-Party Risk
Risk introduced through vendors, contractors, and partner access patterns.
R.14
Policy & Regulatory Compliance
Adherence to cybersecurity rules, standards, and legal obligations over time.
R.15
Human Use of AI
How individuals interact with and apply AI systems at work — a new and growing risk surface.
R.16
Agentic AI
How organisations govern autonomous AI agents operating within the enterprise environment.
R.15 and R.16 are not available in any legacy SAT platform. They exist in Moxso because the system treats human behavior as an intelligence layer — not a training problem. These categories are category-defining, not optional add-ons.

02 — The Closed Loop

Continuous. No manual orchestration required.

From signals to proof.

Moxso runs a continuous closed loop. Behavior is captured as signals, enriched against the Risk Framework and external threat intelligence, converted into individual risk profiles, acted on through training or triggered intervention, and then measured as outcomes. New behavior from those outcomes feeds back as new signals. The loop never stops.

01
Signals
Behavior captured continuously from phishing interactions, training engagement, reporting patterns, and connected controls.
02
Intelligence
Signals enriched with Risk Framework scoring and OSINT engine — live threat landscape mapped to internal behavior.
03
Risk Profiles
Individual and organisational profiles built dynamically. Updated on every new signal. Never static.
04
Action
Two execution paths: continuous adaptive training (system-scheduled) and behavior-triggered intervention (event-driven).
05
Proof
Measurable risk reduction documented as a Human Resilience Score — a trajectory over time, not a snapshot.
Training path
Scheduled by system based on current risk profile. Not by a human calendar.
Intervention path
Triggered by a behavioral event. Enrich → select → assign → update score.
OSINT engine
Live external threat intelligence mapped continuously to internal signals.
Risk score update
Every signal updates the individual profile in real time. No scheduled recalculation.
Board output
Human Resilience Score — a single defensible number with a trajectory the board can read.
03 — Signal Types

What the system captures.

Moxso processes signals across six categories. Each signal type feeds one or more risk dimensions in the framework. The system does not require manual tagging or curation — signal enrichment is automated against the Risk Framework and OSINT inputs.

Signal type What it captures Risk categories fed
Phishing interactions Clicks, non-clicks, reports, response time, repeated patterns across simulation campaigns R.01, R.08, R.09
Training engagement Completion behavior, time-on-task, knowledge assessment results, re-engagement patterns R.02, R.14
Breach exposure data Individual and organisational credential exposures from external breach monitoring R.04, R.11
OSINT threat inputs Live phishing trends, industry-specific targeting patterns, regulatory changes — mapped to R.01–R.16 All categories
Behavioral patterns Longitudinal risk trajectory per individual — how behavior changes over time across the framework R.01–R.16
Connected control outputs API outputs from integrated enterprise security tools — endpoint, identity, SIEM R.04, R.05, R.06
04 — Integrations & Stack

Two required. Everything else is optional.

What it connects to.
What it requires.

Moxso requires two integrations to function. Everything else is optional and extends capability. The required integrations handle identity provisioning and phishing simulation delivery — both are standard enterprise connectors with documented setup paths and typical completion times measured in hours, not weeks.

Required must have
Microsoft Entra ID / Azure AD
Required
Identity & directory
SSO and automated user provisioning. Enables: automatic user sync from directory, role-based risk profiling, organisational structure mapping for department-level analysis, and onboarding/offboarding triggers.

Setup time: typically 2–4 hours with IT admin access.
Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace
Required
Email & communication layer
Phishing simulation delivery and behavioral signal capture from email interaction patterns. Enables: realistic phishing scenarios delivered from trusted send infrastructure, signal capture from click and report events, and integration with email reporting buttons.

Setup time: typically 1–3 hours with Exchange or Google admin access.
Optional extends capability
SIEM & Security tools
Optional
Push risk intelligence outbound
Moxso can push risk scores, intervention records, and proof artifacts to existing SIEM or security tooling via API. For organisations that want Moxso's output visible inside their existing security operations stack.
HRIS
Optional
Role-based profiling at scale
HRIS connection enables department-level risk segmentation, automatic profile updates on role changes, and onboarding-triggered baseline assessments. Most useful for organisations with 500+ employees or significant role churn.
Drata / Vanta
Optional
Compliance evidence integration
Native integration with Drata and Vanta compliance platforms. Moxso risk data feeds directly into compliance evidence packages — audit-ready documentation without manual export or reconciliation.
Moxso does not require agents, endpoint software, or network-level access. The integration footprint is deliberately minimal — identity, email, and optionally HRIS. Everything else is additive.
05 — Data Architecture & Privacy

Where data lives.
How it's processed.

Moxso processes human behavioural data entirely within EU infrastructure. This is a design decision, not a configuration option. Data residency, processing boundaries, and retention policy are documented below for DPO review and procurement evaluation.

Primary infrastructure
DigitalOcean — EU region
All primary data processing and storage occurs within DigitalOcean EU infrastructure. No primary data leaves EU jurisdiction under any operational condition.
Secondary processing
AWS EMEA SARL
Secondary processing infrastructure via AWS EMEA SARL. Covered under EU data protection framework. No US-region processing of customer data.
Operational tooling
Google Workspace EU · HubSpot
Operational tooling runs on EU-configured Google Workspace. HubSpot used for customer data management under standard data processing agreement.
Continuous monitoring
Vanta
Continuous compliance monitoring via Vanta. Real-time evidence collection for ISO 27001, SOC 2, and GDPR obligations. Audit-ready evidence available on request.
Personal data processed
Behavioural signals only
Moxso processes behavioural signal data tied to employee identifiers. No content scanning, no keystroke logging, no surveillance. Legal basis: legitimate interest in organisational security under GDPR Article 6(1)(f).
Subprocessors
Full list available on request
Complete subprocessor registry provided on request as part of procurement documentation. All subprocessors operate under appropriate data processing agreements and EU adequacy frameworks.

07 — Deployment

From zero to board-ready output in 30 days.

What deployment
actually looks like.

A standard Moxso deployment is complete within four weeks. The first two weeks are integration and configuration — handled jointly with Moxso's technical onboarding team. Weeks three and four are live signal capture and first output review. The IT team's active involvement is concentrated in week one.

Week 1
Integration & Configuration
  • Microsoft Entra ID / Azure AD connection
  • User directory sync and provisioning
  • M365 or Google Workspace email setup
  • SSO configuration and access testing
  • Organisational structure mapping
IT team active involvement: 4–8 hours total
Week 2
Baseline & First Signals
  • Initial phishing simulation campaign
  • Baseline risk profiles populating
  • OSINT engine mapping to internal structure
  • First risk category scores generated
  • Admin dashboard access configured
First risk signals visible in platform
Weeks 3–4
Live Operation
  • Continuous adaptive training active
  • Behavior-triggered interventions live
  • Individual risk profiles updating
  • Onboarding review with Moxso team
  • Optional: HRIS and SIEM integration
System operating fully autonomously
Day 30
First Board-Ready Output
  • Human Resilience Score established
  • Risk trajectory baseline set
  • Department-level risk breakdown available
  • NIS2 evidence package ready for review
  • First board report exportable
Defensible risk number available for board
After week one, the platform operates without requiring IT involvement. There is no ongoing administration burden. User provisioning, training assignment, intervention triggers, and risk score updates are all system-driven.
